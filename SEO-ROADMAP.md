# Horizon Physician Services — SEO Keyword & Content Roadmap

A practical plan for what each page should rank for and what to publish next. Your audience is **US healthcare providers and practice administrators** searching for outsourced billing and revenue cycle help.

---

| Home (`/`) | medical billing company | revenue cycle management company, RCM services, outsourced medical billing |
| `medical-billing.html` | medical billing services | outsourced medical billing, medical billing company, physician billing services |

## 2. On-page checklist (apply to every page)

For each page, confirm:
- Primary keyword appears in the **title tag**, **H1**, **first 100 words**, and **at least one subheading**.
- The page answers the searcher's intent (what the service is, what's included, benefits, FAQ).
- **Internal links** point to related service pages (already done via "Related services" + nav dropdown).
- A clear **call-to-action** (Schedule a Consultation) appears at least twice.
- Unique meta description under ~155 characters (done).
- Structured data present (Service + FAQ schema — done).

> To change service-page wording, edit `build-services.js` and regenerate, or ask me. To change homepage copy, use the admin → Pages & Text.

## 3. Content gaps & opportunities

These are pages/sections that would capture searches you currently can't rank for:

1. **Specialty pages** (highest opportunity). Many searches are specialty-specific: "medical billing for cardiology," "behavioral health billing services," "orthopedic billing company." A handful of specialty landing pages (e.g. Cardiology, Behavioral Health, Pain Management, Internal Medicine) would each rank for their niche. Highest effort, highest reward.
2. **Comparison / decision content**: "in-house vs outsourced medical billing," "how to choose a medical billing company," "medical billing company cost." These catch buyers in research mode.
3. **Location intent**: if you want US-state visibility, lightweight state pages ("medical billing services in Texas") can help — but only worthwhile if you can make each genuinely useful, not thin duplicates.
4. **A real, indexable blog** (in progress) — the single best ongoing ranking engine. See the calendar below.

## 5. Off-page & authority (ongoing)

- **Reviews**: ask satisfied clients for Google reviews — the biggest lever for local/trust ranking. Even 5–10 helps.
- **Citations / directories**: list the business consistently (same name, phone, site) on healthcare and B2B directories (Clutch, GoodFirms, Healthgrades-adjacent B2B lists, LinkedIn company page).
- **Backlinks**: guest posts on healthcare-admin blogs, partnerships, and being cited as a billing resource. Quality over quantity.
- **LinkedIn**: post the blog articles from the company page to build signals and referral traffic.

---

## 6. What to monitor (monthly, in Search Console + GA4)

- **Search Console → Performance**: impressions and clicks per page and per query. Watch which keywords you're appearing for and your average position. Pages stuck on page 2 (positions 11–20) are your best optimization targets.
- **Search Console → Pages**: confirm all pages stay "Indexed."
- **Search Console → Enhancements**: FAQ rich results appearing.
- **GA4** (once set up): sessions, traffic sources, top landing pages, and consultation-form conversions.

**Rule of thumb:** SEO compounds over 3–6+ months. Publish consistently, keep pages updated, earn reviews and links, and watch positions climb.
